Derivative Rules#

  • Constant Multiple Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}[c f(x)] = c f'(x)\)

  • Sum Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)+g(x)] = f'(x)+g'(x)\)

  • Difference Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)-g(x)] = f'(x)-g'(x)\)

  • Product Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(x)g(x)] = f'(x)g(x)+f(x)g'(x)\)

  • Quotient Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}\left[\frac{f(x)}{g(x)}\right] = \frac{g(x)f'(x)-f(x)g'(x)} {(g(x))^2}\)

  • Chain Rule: \(\frac{d}{dx}[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))\cdot g'(x)\)

Derivatives for Machine Learning and Computer Vision#

Example 1: Polynomial Function#

Problem: Find the derivative of \(f(x)=3x^4+2x^2-5x+7\)

Solution

\(\begin{aligned} f'(x) &= \frac{d}{dx}(3x^4+2x^2-5x+7) \\ &= \frac{d}{dx}(3x^4)+\frac{d}{dx}(2x^2)+\frac{d}{dx}(-5x)+\frac{d}{dx}(7) \\ &= 12x^3+4x-5+0 \\ &= 12x^3+4x-5 \end{aligned}\)

Answer: \(\boxed{f'(x)=12x^3+4x-5}\)

Example 2: Chain Rule#

Find

\( \frac{d}{dx}(x^2+1)^5 \)

Solution:

\(\begin{aligned} &\text{Let u} = x^2+1, \\ & Then f(x) = u^5 \\ & \text{Differentiate outer function first.} \\ & \frac{d}{du}(u^5) = 5u^4 \\ & \text{Differentiate inner function.} \\ & \frac{du}{dx} = 2x \\ & \text{Apply Chain Rule.} \\ & \frac{df}{dx} = 5u^4 \cdot 2x \\ & \text{Substitute back.} \\ & \boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x^2+1)^5 = 10x(x^2+1)^4} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 3: Exponential Function#

Find

\( \frac{d}{dx}(e^{3x}) \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Use } \frac{d}{dx}(e^u)=e^u\frac{du}{dx} \\ & \text{Let } u = 3x \\ & \frac{du}{dx} = 3 \\ & \text{Apply the Chain Rule.} \\ & \frac{d}{dx}(e^{3x}) = e^u \cdot 3 \\ & \text{Substitute back.} \\ & \boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(e^{3x}) = 3e^{3x}} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 4: Logarithmic Function#

Find

\( \frac{d}{dx}\ln(x^2+1) \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Use } \frac{d}{dx}\ln(u)=\frac{u'}{u} \\ & \text{Let } u = x^2+1 \\ & \frac{du}{dx} = 2x \\ & \text{Substitute into the formula.} \\ & \boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\ln(x^2+1)=\frac{2x}{x^2+1}} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 5: Sigmoid Function#

Find derivative of

\( \sigma(x)=\frac{1}{1+e^{-x}} \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Rewrite as } \sigma(x)=(1+e^{-x})^{-1} \\ & \text{Differentiate using the Chain Rule.} \\ & \sigma'(x)=-(1+e^{-x})^{-2}(-e^{-x}) \\ & \text{Simplify.} \\ & \sigma'(x)=\frac{e^{-x}}{(1+e^{-x})^2} \\ & \text{Express in terms of } \sigma(x). \\ & \boxed{\sigma'(x)=\sigma(x)(1-\sigma(x))} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 6: Mean Squared Error (MSE)#

Find

\( \frac{dL}{d\hat y} \)

given

\( L=(y-\hat y)^2 \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Let } u = y-\hat y \\ & \text{Then } L=u^2 \\ & \text{Differentiate outer function first.} \\ & \frac{dL}{du}=2u \\ & \text{Differentiate inner function.} \\ & \frac{du}{d\hat y}=-1 \\ & \text{Apply the Chain Rule.} \\ & \frac{dL}{d\hat y}=2u(-1) \\ & \text{Substitute back.} \\ & \boxed{\frac{dL}{d\hat y}=-2(y-\hat y)=2(\hat y-y)} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 7: Linear Regression Gradient#

Find

\( \frac{dL}{dw} \)

given

\( \hat y=wx+b \)

and

\( L=(y-\hat y)^2 \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Substitute } \hat y=wx+b \\ & L=(y-(wx+b))^2 \\ & \text{Let } u=y-(wx+b) \\ & \text{Then } L=u^2 \\ & \text{Differentiate outer function first.} \\ & \frac{dL}{du}=2u \\ & \text{Differentiate inner function.} \\ & \frac{du}{dw}=-x \\ & \text{Apply the Chain Rule.} \\ & \frac{dL}{dw}=2u(-x) \\ & \text{Substitute back.} \\ & \boxed{\frac{dL}{dw}=-2x(y-\hat y)} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Example 8: Softmax Cross Entropy (Simplified)#

Find

\( \frac{dL}{dp} \)

given

\( L=-\log(p) \)

Solution#

\(\begin{aligned} & \text{Use } \frac{d}{dp}\ln(p)=\frac{1}{p} \\ & \text{Differentiate.} \\ & \frac{dL}{dp}=-\frac{1}{p} \\ & \boxed{\frac{dL}{dp}=-\frac{1}{p}} \\ \end{aligned}\)

Key ML Derivatives Summary#

Quick Memory List for ML#

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(c)=0}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x)=1}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1}}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)=-\frac{1}{x^2}}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)=e^x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(a^x)=a^x\ln(a)}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)=\frac{1}{x}}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\sin x)=\cos x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\cos x)=-\sin x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\tan x)=\sec^2x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\arcsin x)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\arctan x)=\frac{1}{1+x^2}}\\ \)

\(\textbf{Product Rule:}\\ \) \(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(fg)=f'g+fg'}\\ \)

\(\textbf{Quotient Rule:}\\ \) \(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{f}{g}\right)=\frac{gf'-fg'}{g^2}}\\ \)

\(\textbf{Chain Rule:}\\ \) \(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\big[f(g(x))\big]=f'(g(x))\,g'(x)}\\ \)

\(\textbf{Gradient Descent:}\\ \) \(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x^2)=2x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(y-\hat y)^2=2(y-\hat y)(-\hat y')} \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x^n)=nx^{n-1}}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(e^x)=e^x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x)=\frac1x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\sin x=\cos x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}\cos x=-\sin x}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\sigma'(x)=\sigma(x)(1-\sigma(x))}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{d}{d\hat y}(y-\hat y)^2=2(\hat y-y)}\\ \)

\(\boxed{\frac{dL}{dw}=-2x(y-\hat y)} \\ \)

These derivatives alone cover roughly 80–90% of the mathematics encountered in Linear Regression, Logistic Regression, Neural Networks, Backpropagation, Deep Learning, and Computer Vision.
